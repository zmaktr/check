Simona Ravera

Director of Sales, Life Sciences & Healthcare Solutions, EMEA; responsible for the CEIV Pharma certification program

With about 35% of their overall business in pharma transportation, it was important to BDP International to obtain a certification of their processes, so they could reassure their customers they had them covered in the constantly changing, logistically difficult situation caused by the pandemic.

Half the effort required for certification is in training, and they needed to train 20 people from multiple areas of the business, at the same time, so everyone understood the entire chain of responsibility and their part in it. These people had varying levels of experience: from beginners to decades in the industry. And they were located in three different cities in three different countries, each being an important pharma transportation center: Milan, Brussels and New York.

IATA Training offered the CEIV Pharma certification course in a LIVE virtual in-house training format, working with BDP International to find the best time for sessions (4pm Central European Time) to enable the entire team on both sides of the Atlantic to do the training together. The IATA trainer then adapted the teaching methodology to meet the needs of the participants with their varying levels of experience and different roles, keeping their attention through a high level of interaction. Having completed the training, the BDP employees knew what they needed to put into place to obtain the certification, and how to do it quickly. The training taught them the theory, and allowed them to practice it.

A training certification success story

"Our staff are more confident in their work because what they learn is more relevant to their everyday jobs, and they are sure it applies for every airline. This in turn enables us to provide higher quality service, as well as reducing hazards in operations."

Mohamed Hassanain

Quality and Process Manager

As the leading Egyptian cargo service provider, CACC Cargolinx serves more than 20 of the top cargo air freight companies in the world when they visit Cairo International Airport.

To provide exceptional ground handling services, focused on the carrier, CACC Cargolinx was training its personnel in the individual requirements of every single airline - a challenging situation for both Cargolinx and the trainees. Having received Training Validation Program certification for ULD handling in 2019, they wanted to focus on dangerous goods handling in compliance with the DGR, using IATA's services to obtain guidance on standardizing their training through competency-based training and assessment (CBTA).

The consultants responsible for IATA Training Certification helped CACC Cargolinx understand CBTA, and how to implement it. The pilot assessment allowed them to test their new training approach, identifying the gaps and necessary corrective action. In three months, the cargo service provider had streamlined its dangerous goods training, giving existing staff greater confidence that they're doing the right thing, and enabling new staff to be operational more quickly. Feedback from existing customers has been very positive, with every carrier approving the industry-standard training, and CACC Cargolinx are also confident the certification will make their services even more attractive to potential new customers.
